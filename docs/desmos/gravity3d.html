3D Gravity Simulation - Documentation

Summary

This is a simulation of the movements of two bodies of given masses and starting positions in a 3D space. Note: Desmos uses imprecise floating point maths - the behaviour will not perfectly match what should theoretically be expected.

Usage

  • To run the simulation, click the metronome with the play button in the top left.
  • To pause, click it again.
  • To reset, click the arrow next to the reset function - the second level of the list on the left.

Explanations of Individual Variables and Functions

Variables

  • tscale - Short for time scale; controls the speed of the simulation.
  • camdist - Short for camera distance. Effectively controls the z position of the camera.
  • x0, y0, z0, x1, y1, z1 - The x, y and z positions of each body.
  • r0, r1 - The radii of the two bodies. Altering this does not affect the mass - mass must be manually altered (i.e. density is not constant).
  • v0, v1 - The velocities of the two bodies, as a list in the form of [x, y, z] These can be altered while the simulation is not running to give either body an initial velocity.
  • m0, m1 - The masses of the two bodies.
  • r - The distance between the two bodies, set every tick.
  • f - The force that should be exerted on both bodies as a scalar, set every tick.
  • vector0, vector1 - Vectors of magnitude 1, stored as lists in the form of [x, y, z], that represent the directions of the forces that should be applied to the two bodies.
  • C - The x and y position of the centre of mass. The z is unnecessary as the centre of mass is only for a visual purpose.
  • xcentre, ycentre, zcentre - The x, y and z positions of the centre of mass, used for the label on point C.
  • B1, B2 - Points in the centres of the two bodies, so that they can be labeled.
  • xB1, yB1, zB1, xB2, yB2, zB2 - The x, y, and z positions of the two bodies, to one decimal place. Used for the labels on the two bodies.

Functions

  • a(d) - Short for accelerate (i.e. accelerate the two bodies) but effectively serves as a master function, running every tick and doing the majority of the calculations. Its full responsibilities are as follows:
    • Calculate the distance between the two bodies.
    • Calculate the force that should be applied to the two bodies, as a scalar.
    • Calculate the direction in which this force should be applied to each body.
    • Calculate what the consequent acceleration on each body should be, and hence adjust their velocities.
    • Move the two bodies according to their new velocities.
    The function takes d (delta time) as a parameter so that the simulation runs at a consistent speed even if the duration of individual ticks varies.
  • reset - Resets the simulation to a preset state.
  • distfunc(dist) - When calculating the force of gravity, the force is equal to the reciprocal of this function of the distance between the two bodies. So, the function returns dist2 by default as real gravity is inversely proportional to the square of the distances between the two bodies.
